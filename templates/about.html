How It Works

Our system combines two forecasting approaches:

Regional Predictors (70% weight) - Short-range 12-48 hours:
  • Winnipeg, MB - Alberta Clipper detection
  • Thunder Bay, ON - Regional systems (r=0.468)
  • Duluth & Marquette - Lake Effect detection
  • Green Bay & Iron Mountain - Regional patterns
Global Predictors (30% weight) - Long-range 5-7 days:
  • Sapporo, Japan - 6-day lead (r=0.120)
  • Chamonix, France - 5-day lead (r=0.115)
  • Irkutsk, Russia - 7-day lead (r=0.074)

All correlations are statistically significant (p < 0.001, 99.9% confidence).

Event Type Detection

The system identifies the primary mechanism driving each snow event:

  • Lake Effect (61% of events) - Cold air over Lake Superior
  • Alberta Clippers (25% of events) - Fast-moving Canadian systems
  • Regional Systems (2% of events) - Local low-pressure
  • Global Patterns (4% of events) - Large-scale atmospheric patterns
